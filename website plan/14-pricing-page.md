# Muslim Will Pricing Page

## Page Role

The pricing page should make the offer feel clear, complete, and trustworthy.

Its job is to:

- show the exact price publicly
- explain what is included in the flat-fee package
- make the value of the 4-document package obvious
- compare Muslim Will calmly against free templates and traditional law firms
- reduce anxiety around hidden fees, hourly billing, and unclear next steps
- move ready users toward `Start Your Will`

This page should feel transparent, premium, and legally careful.

## Strategic Decision

Show the price publicly.

The pricing page should not hide the price behind a consultation. Muslim families may assume Islamic estate planning is expensive or lawyer-hourly by default. A clear price helps reduce hesitation.

## Visual Direction

### Pricing Hero

Use one strong price card near the top. Do not bury the price.

### Inclusions

Use a clean checklist or 2-column grid. The four documents should be visually prominent.

### Comparison Table

Keep it restrained. Do not make the comparison feel like an attack on lawyers or templates.

### Color Use

- deep green for headings and trust anchors
- cream for page rhythm
- gold for the price, separators, and small highlights

### Design Warning

Do not make the pricing page look like a SaaS pricing table with multiple confusing tiers. The strongest message is one complete package.
