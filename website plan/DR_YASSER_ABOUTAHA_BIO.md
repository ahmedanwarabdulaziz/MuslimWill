# Dr. Yasser Aboutaha

## Role
**Executive Adviser, Muslim Will**

## Short Bio
Dr. Yasser Aboutaha is Executive Adviser at Muslim Will, where he helps build trusted relationships with families, masajid, charities, and community leaders. His work brings community credibility, practical communication, and partnership depth to the Muslim Will mission across Canada.

## Full Bio
Dr. Yasser Aboutaha brings a rare combination of Muslim community presence and professional experience to Muslim Will. Trained in dentistry, he has spent more than fifteen years working across project management, international marketing, public speaking, event leadership, mentoring, life coaching, and fundraising in service of Muslim communities and mission-driven organizations across Canada. His career has not been built in abstraction. It has been shaped in the settings where trust is earned directly: masajid, community events, charitable campaigns, family-centered initiatives, and long-term institutional relationships.

As a co-founder of Masjid Fundraising, Dr. Yasser has worked directly with masajid, charities, and NGOs since 2009, helping organizations communicate more clearly, build stronger community trust, and move people from goodwill to meaningful action. His work has included fundraising campaigns, Islamic lectures, youth programs, public speaking engagements, community gatherings, and family-oriented initiatives designed to support real needs with practical clarity. He is known not only for energy and presence, but for warmth, follow-through, and an ability to speak to communities in a way that feels grounded rather than performative.

That experience gives Dr. Yasser a distinctive role at Muslim Will. Preparing a will is not only a legal or technical task. For many families, it is also a sensitive community conversation about responsibility, guardianship, inheritance, final wishes, and the kind of clarity they want to leave behind. Dr. Yasser helps Muslim Will meet that conversation in the right way. He serves as a trusted bridge between the organization and the communities it hopes to support, helping families, masajid, charities, NGOs, and community leaders understand why will planning matters and how a clearer pathway can help.

At Muslim Will, he serves as Executive Adviser, helping build meaningful relationships with masajid, charities, NGOs, and community leaders across Canada. His role strengthens the community-facing side of the platform, ensuring that the mission is communicated with trust, empathy, and practical understanding. He helps translate Muslim Will from a professional service into a pathway that families and institutions can approach with greater confidence.

Dr. Yasser's contribution is especially important because the strongest planning pathways are not built on process alone. They are built on trust, communication, and community relevance. His background in fundraising, speaking, partnership-building, and community leadership helps ensure that Muslim Will remains connected to the people it is meant to serve, and that the message stays family-first, responsible, and rooted in real community needs.
