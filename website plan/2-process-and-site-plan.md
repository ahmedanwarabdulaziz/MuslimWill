## Site Goal

Build Muslim Will as a premium, trust-first, conversion-focused website for Muslim families in Canada.

The website should feel:

- calm
- elevated
- family-first
- professionally accountable
- faith-conscious
- modern, not templated

## Strategic Positioning

### Market Position

- launch message: Canada-first
- strongest legal proof point: Ontario
- future-ready direction: North America, including the United States later

### Brand Promise

Muslim Will helps Muslim families move from intention to a properly prepared will through a guided process shaped by legal structure, Islamic inheritance awareness, and clear next steps.

## Tone System

Every page should sound:

- warm, not casual
- precise, not cold
- premium, not corporate
- trustworthy, not dramatic
- faith-conscious, not performative
- clear, not dense

### Tone Rules

- avoid fear-first copy
- avoid generic legal website phrases
- avoid hype or exaggerated claims
- avoid overly technical legal wording in hero sections
- keep the strongest legal nuance lower on the page
- write like a serious service brand with community awareness

## Structural Rules For Every Page

Each major page should follow the same content rhythm when possible.

### Standard Page Structure

1. Hero
   Clear H1, short supporting paragraph, primary CTA
2. Trust Layer
   One short credibility section, stat strip, or positioning line
3. Core Explanation
   Explain the page topic in simple, structured sections
4. Proof / Clarity Layer
   Process, FAQs, authority, or examples
5. Objection Handling
   Clarify what users are usually unsure about
6. CTA Close
   One final clean next step

### Page Writing Formula

Each page should answer:

- what this page is about
- why it matters
- how it works
- what makes it credible
- what the visitor should do next

## CTA System

The website should keep one conversion hierarchy across all pages.

### Primary CTA

`Start Your Will`

### Secondary CTA

`Book a Call`

### Supporting CTAs

- `See How It Works`
- `View Full FAQ`
- `Explore Charitable Legacy`
- `Read Full Bio`
- `Partnership Information`

### CTA Rule

`Start Your Will` should be the strongest action on almost every page.

## SEO And AEO System

### SEO Rules

- one clear primary keyword per page
- one clear H1 per page
- strong H2 hierarchy
- internal links between related pages
- title tags should be keyword-aware and readable
- meta descriptions should sound human, not stuffed

### AEO Rules

Each page should answer real user questions in a direct format.

Use:

- clear first-sentence answers
- concise explanatory follow-up
- FAQ blocks where useful
- schema where relevant

### Core Schema Types

- `Organization`
- `WebSite`
- `FAQPage`
- `Person`
- `BreadcrumbList`
- later where needed: `Service`

## Design Consistency Rules

### Colors

- deep green for trust and structure
- warm cream for softness and rhythm
- gold for disciplined highlights only

### Layout

- spacious sections
- generous vertical rhythm
- clear content hierarchy
- no crowded blocks
- desktop and mobile should both feel premium

### Images

- editorial style
- modern Muslim family life
- refined portraits
- charity imagery only where it is clearly relevant
- no generic legal stock clichés

## Shared Section Rules

### Hero

Every hero should have:

- eyebrow or context label
- strong H1
- short supporting paragraph
- one primary CTA
- optional secondary CTA

### FAQ Use

Use FAQ blocks when:

- the page answers real search questions
- objections are common
- the topic is legally or operationally sensitive

### Final CTA

Every major page should end with:

- one clear action
- one reassurance line
- no visual clutter

## Copy Guardrails

Do:

- say what the platform does
- explain role boundaries clearly
- use Canada-first wording
- use Ontario as proof point where relevant
- keep charity language responsible and grounded

Do not:

- say Muslim Will is a law firm
- say Muslim Will gives fatwas
- make broad U.S. legal claims now
- overstate legal outcomes
- make charity sound like it competes with family responsibility

## Working Definition Of A Professional Page

A page is ready when it has:

- a clear purpose
- a strong H1
- readable structure
- one clear primary CTA
- SEO metadata
- AEO-friendly questions or answers where relevant
- at least one trust layer
- clean internal links
